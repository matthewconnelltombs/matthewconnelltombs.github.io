The Brief

Yahtzee is a dice-based board game that combines luck and strategy. Designed to be played with any number of people, players take turns rolling five dice, aiming to achieve specific dice combinations that will score them the most points. Although simple, Yahtzee has elements of strategy and competition that everyone can enjoy.

Yahtzee is a game I grew up playing with my parents, as it is my mom’s favorite game. I quickly became fond of the game — I was drawn to figuring out which dice roll combinations had the best payoffs, and calculating the probabilities to determine when I should re-evaluate my aim or stay the course. I did this purely out of my love for math (not because I loved to win, obviously…)

I decided to implement one of my favorite childhood games in Python using the pygame and numpy libraries.

The Results

Below we can see the opening screen of the game:

The game displays the possible dice roll combinations you should be aiming for. It calculates the possible scores for each option, as well as the running totals for your upper and lower board scores. The session and all-time high scores are also displayed.

If your game is not going well, feel free to reset the game to have another go. Selection of dice is controlled by the 5 corresponding buttons, with green indicating a die you’d like to reroll, and red indicating hold. Feel free to try your hand at it and see if you can beat my high score!
